Product Description

Originally an East Asian invention, the folding fan was an important accessory for both upper and middle classes. While quite handy for cooling off in a heated ballroom, fans could also be printed with riddles, maps, dance steps â€“ the ideal conversation piece. Finished fans could have European or Asian designs, or be made up of only carved sticks. Making a fan could also be a global effort. Fan sticks of ivory were often imported from China to Europe, where master craftsmen decorated them. Variety in material and design gave Early Americans a lot of choice.
